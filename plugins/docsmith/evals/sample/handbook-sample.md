# The Moment It Clicks

::: anchor
**The core idea:** you don't get hired for what you know — you get hired for
what you can prove you can ship.
:::

You don't lose reviews because you're not careful. You lose them because the
value isn't visible. This chapter is the shortest path from "I reviewed it" to
"I improved it" — using a system that turns effort into evidence.

## A quick story

You open a merge request and your stomach tightens a little. It's not the work
— you could review it — but the gap between what you notice and what you can
explain. A good handbook closes that gap with structure, not heroics.

::: tip
**Quick win:** if you can explain *why* a change is risky in one sentence, you
instantly sound safer than most reviewers.
:::

# How the Loop Works

Each week, pick one concept and attach it to a real change. Build the habit,
prove it works, and write down what changed.

::: do
Read the canonical adapter first — it's the pattern you'll mirror everywhere.
:::

::: dont
Don't copy-paste from a stub; you quietly re-introduce escaping bugs.
:::

::: cheatsheet
Pick one lane, review three changes deeply, evaluate honestly, explain clearly.
The work becomes easier when your notes already contain the answers.
:::
