###### meet the mascot

# Say hi to *Pip*. 🐶

![Pip the Pup, a kawaii dog holding a white envelope with a paw-print stamp](/Users/harry/projects/claude-plugins/plugins/docsmith/skills/make-pdf-workspace/iteration-2/eval-0-kawaii-feature-deck/old_skill/run-1/outputs/mascot-pip.svg)

Pip is your friendly mail-pup. He fetches new letters, wags when a reply lands, and keeps your inbox tidy so you can spend more time with your real pets.

- **Fetches** every new message and drops it gently in your lap.
- **Sorts** vet reminders, treats, and friends into cozy little baskets.
- **Reminds** you, softly, when a furry friend is waiting on a reply.

---

<!-- _class: cards -->

###### helpful tips

# Two tiny *tips* for happy mail.

- **Tag your pets** Add a 🐾 tag to each pet's thread so vet notes, photos, and playdate plans never wander off into the wrong basket.
- **Use Quiet Paws** Flip on *Quiet Paws* in the evening — Pip naps, notifications hush, and your inbox waits politely until morning.

---

<!-- _class: statement caution -->

###### a gentle warning

# One thing to *watch*. 🐾

Auto-fetch is friendly, but it pulls mail every few minutes — even on cellular.

> ⚠️ Heads up: on a small data plan, turn **auto-fetch** to *Wi-Fi only* so Pip doesn't nibble through your gigabytes while you're out walking the dog.

###### for the curious

# A *peek* at the send API. 🦴

Sending a letter through Pawmail is one cozy little call:

```js
await pawmail.send({
  to: "buddy@pawmail.app",
  subject: "Walkies at 5? 🐕",
  body: "Bring the squeaky ball!",
  paw: "🐾"   // optional paw-print stamp
});
```

That's it — Pip carries it the rest of the way.
