<!-- _class: pillars -->

###### Strategy

# Three pillars hold up FY26.

- **Land durable revenue** Shift the mix toward multi-year enterprise commitments and reduce reliance on month-to-month plans.
- **Deepen the platform** Turn point features into an integrated platform customers standardize on, not just adopt.
- **Operate with discipline** Hold spend growth below revenue growth and expand operating margin every quarter.

---

<!-- _class: cards3 -->

###### What's working

# Three bets that paid off.

- **Self-serve onboarding** Cut time-to-first-value from 14 days to under 2, lifting trial-to-paid conversion by 11 points.
- **Usage-based pricing** Aligned cost with value; expansion revenue now outpaces new-logo revenue two to one.
- **Partner ecosystem** 60+ integrations turned Acme into the default hub for mid-market operations teams.

###### Platform architecture

# One platform, four layers.

![Acme platform architecture: customer channels feed an edge gateway, which routes to core services, backed by a data platform and an observability and security mesh.](/Users/harry/projects/pangaealabs-claude-plugins-marketplace/plugins/docsmith/examples/corporate-deck/diagrams/architecture.svg)

###### How we ship

# From idea to production in three steps.

- **Discover** Customer interviews and usage data set the quarterly bet before a line of code is written.
- **Build** Small teams ship behind flags weekly, validating with design partners as they go.
- **Scale** Graduated rollouts, SLAs, and on-call ownership take a feature from beta to GA.

<!-- _class: versus -->

###### The shift

# From feature vendor to platform.

- **Before** Customers bought individual features, churned when a competitor matched one, and renewed on price.
- **After** Customers standardize on the platform, expand into new modules, and renew on the switching cost of leaving.

---

<!-- _class: procon -->

###### The pricing decision

# Should we move fully usage-based?

- **In favor** Revenue tracks value delivered, expansion is automatic, and customers never overpay for idle seats.
- **Against** Revenue is harder to forecast quarter to quarter, and procurement teams prefer fixed, predictable line items.
